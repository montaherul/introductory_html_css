Welcome to Bangladesh


Cox's Bazar

Cox’s Bazar, in southeastern Bangladesh, boasts the world’s longest unbroken sea beach, stretching 120 km along the Bay of Bengal. Known as the “Tourist Capital,” it offers golden sand, rolling waves, and beautiful sunsets.

Bandarban

Bandarban is a hill district rich in natural beauty, waterfalls, and tribal culture. It’s home to Nilgiri Hills, Nafakhum Falls, and peaceful Buddhist monasteries.

Saint Martin's Island

Saint Martin’s Island is Bangladesh’s only coral island, famous for its crystal-clear waters, coconut trees, and a serene environment ideal for relaxation.

Teesta Barrage Rangpur

The Teesta Barrage is an irrigation and flood control project in Rangpur, providing a scenic view of the mighty Teesta River.

Rajshahi Division

Paharpur’s Somapura Mahavihara in Rajshahi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing Bangladesh’s ancient Buddhist heritage.

Bangladesh Agricultural University

Located in Mymensingh, BAU is the largest institution for agricultural studies in Bangladesh, contributing greatly to research and innovation.

The Capital Dhaka

Dhaka, the capital city, is a vibrant hub of culture, history, and modern development, known for Lalbagh Fort, Ahsan Manzil, and bustling markets.

Khulna

Khulna is the gateway to the Sundarbans mangrove forest, home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and unique biodiversity.

Kuakata Beach

Kuakata, known as the “Daughter of the Sea,” is one of the few beaches in the world where both sunrise and sunset can be seen over the ocean.

Chittagong Port

Chittagong is Bangladesh’s largest seaport and a major commercial hub, blending trade importance with natural beauty.
